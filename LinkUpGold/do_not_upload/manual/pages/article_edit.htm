Editing an existing article - article_edit.php

This script is used to edit an existing article.

To show the form where an article owner can login to edit an article should be the script called as article_edit.php. This login form is applicable only for article owners who are not registered users. Registered users can see a list of their articles inside users area, they can edit each of these articles without the request to enter an additional password.

The login form is based on template article_edit_login.html. Used variables:

URL of your site.

If the article owner just asked to send an article password to his/her email, it shows this info.

If the article owner just asked to send an article password to his/her email, it shows number of the selected article.

If the article owner doesn't know info about the article which he/she wants to edit, the script can show a list of all articles associated to an entered email address. There is a field to enter email in the login form. The list of articles that are associated to an email is based on template article_edit_list.html - the list of articles associated with the entered email. This list contains links to article details page, link to a login form to edit it and a article to password reminder. Each of the lines is based on template article_edit_list.txt.

Once an article has been edited, multiple states can occur, depending on your configuration. These are:

  • You selected that it has to inform admin when a link has been edited. In this case you receive an email by using template article_admin.txt.
  • You selected that updated articles must be reviewed by an admin. In this case the article is waiting in Administration => Queue. Once you approved it, the article owner receives an email based on template article_approved.txt.
  • You selected that edited articles are immediately online. In this case the old data has been deleted and the edited article is instantly visible on public pages.
